**From D2 (Clinical Study Report):**

**From D3 (FDA Correspondence):**

### Explanation:

For a clinical trial to meet its primary endpoint with statistical significance, the p-value must be **less than or equal to** the pre-specified alpha threshold (typically 0.05).

- P-value = 0.08
- Required threshold = 0.05
- 0.08 > 0.05 = **FAILED**

While Drug X showed a numerical trend favoring treatment (50% relative reduction), this difference could reasonably be attributed to chance (8% probability), which exceeds the acceptable 5% threshold for declaring statistical significance in clinical trials.

**Note:** D1 (press release) does not address this question at all, omitting any mention of statistical significance—a critical and misleading omission given that the trial actually failed its primary endpoint.
