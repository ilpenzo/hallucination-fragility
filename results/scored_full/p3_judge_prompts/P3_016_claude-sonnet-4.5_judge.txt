For a clinical trial to meet its primary endpoint with statistical significance, the p-value must be **less than or equal to** the pre-specified alpha threshold (typically 0.05).

- P-value = 0.08
- Required threshold = 0.05
- 0.08 > 0.05 = **FAILED**

A p-value of 0.08 means there is an 8% probability that the observed difference could have occurred by chance alone, which exceeds the acceptable 5% risk threshold. Therefore, the results are not considered statistically significant, and the trial failed to demonstrate efficacy by regulatory standards.

**Note:** Document D1 (press release) misleadingly omits this critical information, but the authoritative technical documents (D2 and D3) are clear and consistent on this point.
